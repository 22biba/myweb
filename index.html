Our History

From Early Beginnings to International Success

Albanian Football has a rich history dating back to the early 20th century when it was introduced by Christian missionaries in Shkodër. The establishment of the Football Association of Albania (FSHF) in 1930 marked a pivotal moment, and the country gained FIFA and UEFA affiliation. The national team has achieved notable successes, including winning the Balkan Nations' Cup in 1946 and qualifying for the UEFA Euro 2016. Albanian clubs, such as KF Tirana and KF Flamutari Vlora, have also made their mark in European competitions, reaching the Round of 16. Despite political obstacles during the communist era, the post-communist era has seen increased participation and support from UEFA, contributing to the growth and development of Albanian football.

Early Beginnings: Football was introduced to Albania by Christian missionaries who played the sport in Shkodër in the early 20th century. The game quickly gained popularity among locals, paving the way for the establishment of organized football in the country.

Founding of FSHF: In 1930, the Football Association of Albania (FSHF) was founded, representing a pivotal moment in the development of Albanian football. This laid the groundwork for future growth and international recognition.

International Recognition: Albania's affiliation with FIFA in 1932 marked its official entry into the international football community. It allowed the Albanian national team to participate in various competitions and fostered a sense of national pride.

Historic Successes: One of the early highlights in Albanian football was the national team's victory in the Balkan Nations' Cup in 1946. This achievement solidified Albania's reputation in regional football.

UEFA Membership: In 1954, the FSHF became a founding member of the Union of European Football Associations (UEFA). This membership opened doors to more European competitions and opportunities for Albanian clubs and the national team.

UEFA Euro 2016 Qualification: Albania's historic qualification for the UEFA Euro 2016 was a remarkable achievement. The national team's journey through the qualifiers and their presence in the tournament captured the hearts of football fans worldwide.

Club Success in Europe: Albanian clubs, including KF Tirana and KF Flamutari Vlora, made significant progress in European competitions. Their appearances in the Round of 16 in various tournaments showcased the quality of Albanian football on a broader stage.

Challenges and Resilience: Despite political obstacles during the communist era, Albanian football persevered. Clubs occasionally faced politically-motivated withdrawals and bans from UEFA, but their resilience and commitment to the sport remained unwavering.

Post-Communist Era: The post-communist era saw a new era of development for Albanian football, with increased support from UEFA. This support played a vital role in the growth and modernization of the sport in the country, enabling Albanian clubs and the national team to compete more effectively on the international stage.
